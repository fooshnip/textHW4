Toy Story

The Storyboard

The following graphic was created in D3 and data parsed from a raw movie script of Toy Story, found from The Internet Movie Script Database. It shows the actual flow of dialogue in the movie over time. The buttons below will trigger an animation that will play through the entire script! Mouseover the chart to see the character's quotes over time.

Play the Movie! Stop the Animation!

The X-axis represents the index in which the character spoke. The circles are only sized for easier mouse-over functionality and size and overlap carry no particular importance (though overlap suggests a lot of activity in succession!).



The Neighborhood Map

Using the raw text data as seen above, we also clustered characters based on similarity of words and language. For comparisons, we utilized a metric called Simpson's distance to quantify the difference. Using a technique called multi-dimensional scaling, we are able to project these distances onto a two-dimensional space.

      Key Takeaways!

The process grouped the main characters together, green circle, likely due to more frequent speaking parts and consistent use of similar proper names. More interesting, the red circle represents characters that are more authoritative. Their use of command-like language drew them closer together.



The Story Arc

Lastly, we utilized sentiment analysis at the word level to try and get a feel for the moving flow of emotion during the movie. We removed stop words, stemmed words to normalize the language, and we also removed punctuation. We ran a moving average comparing movie text against a positive and negative sentiment word list. Lastly, we applied a smoother to create a story arc to best capture overall trends in the movie.
