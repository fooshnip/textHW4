Toy Story

The Storyboard

The following graphic was created in D3 and data parsed from a raw movie script of Toy Story, found from The Internet Movie Script Database. It shows the actual flow of dialogue in the movie over time. The buttons below will trigger an animation that will play through the entire script! Mouseover the chart to see the character's quotes over time.

The X-axis represents the index in which the character spoke. The circles are only sized for easier mouse-over functionality and size and overlap carry no particular importance (though overlap suggests a lot of activity in succession!).

The Neighborhood Map

Using the raw text pulled from the scripts, we clustered the characters based on the type/content of language that they used throughout the movie. To compare, we calculated the difference between characters using Simpson's distance. We then used multi-dimensional scaling to project the characters onto a two-dimensional space. The closer together two characters are, the more similar their speaking patterns are.

      Key Takeaways!

Most of the main characters were placed together on the left, with ancilliary characters spreading out along the right side. In the green circle, we have Woody and Buzz (the two protagonists) very close together. Additionally, many of Andy's other toys (such as Mr. Potato Head, Hamm, Bo Peep, and Slinky) are in the cluster, along with Andy and his family members.

Sid, the movie's main antagonist, shows up in the red circle. Looking at the other characters in the cluster, it is likely that the underlying factor is the constant use of imperative commands ("Stop!", "Halt!", "Go away!", ...) as these characters tell other characters what to do. It also might explain why Woody and Buzz are closer to this cluster than the other characters in the green circle--as the protagonist, they are constantly making plans and directing other toys.



The Story Arc

Lastly, we utilized sentiment analysis at the word level to try and get a feel for the moving flow of emotion during the movie. We removed stop words, stemmed words to normalize the language, and we also removed punctuation. We ran a moving average comparing movie text against a positive and negative sentiment word list. Lastly, we applied a smoother to create a story arc to best capture overall trends in the movie.
